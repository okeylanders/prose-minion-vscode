# Expression Alternatives

This guide provides alternatives to the word "expression" when referring to someone's facial appearance or demeanor. The goal is to create variety and specificity in describing what shows on a character's face, avoiding repetitive use of "expression" while maintaining clarity and impact.

---

## Show, Don't Tell Examples

### Instead of: "Her expression was unreadable"

**Try:**

- Her face gave nothing away.
- She wore a mask of perfect neutrality.
- Her features remained carefully arranged.
- The landscape of her face was still as glass.
- Her countenance betrayed nothing.
- She kept her thoughts locked behind impassive features.
- Her face was a closed book.
- The careful blankness of her features revealed nothing.

---

## Specific Techniques

### Using Body Parts Instead
- "Her eyes carried the weight of exhaustion" (instead of "Her expression was tired")
- "His mouth formed a hard line" (instead of "His expression was stern")
- "Her brow furrowed with concern" (instead of "Her expression was worried")
- "The corners of his eyes crinkled" (instead of "His expression was amused")

### Using Action Instead
- "She arranged her features into polite interest" (instead of "She wore an interested expression")
- "He schooled his face into neutrality" (instead of "He kept his expression neutral")
- "She let her guard drop" (instead of "Her expression became vulnerable")
- "He couldn't hide what showed on his face" (instead of "His expression gave him away")

### Using Comparison/Metaphor
- "Her face was an open book" (instead of "Her expression was readable")
- "He wore his thoughts like a neon sign" (instead of "His expression was obvious")
- "Her features were a locked vault" (instead of "Her expression was guarded")
- "His face was a storm warning" (instead of "His expression was threatening")
