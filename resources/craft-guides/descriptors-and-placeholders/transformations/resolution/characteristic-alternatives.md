# Characteristic Alternatives

This guide provides alternatives to using the word "characteristic" to describe a character's signature traits. The goal is to "show, don't tell" by integrating these traits into the narrative through action, dialogue, and description.

---

## Synonyms and Adjacent Terms

### Signature/Defining Traits
- signature
- typical
- usual
- classic
- quintessential
- distinctive
- particular
- peculiar
- unique to him/her
- representative

### Habitual/Recurring Actions
- habitually
- as always
- true to form
- predictably
- as was his/her habit
- as he/she often did
- in his/her usual fashion
- consistently
- reliably

### Inherent Qualities
- innate
- inherent
- natural
- intrinsic
- ingrained
- deep-seated
- constitutional
- essential


## Show Dont Tell Examples

### Instead of: "her characteristic poise"

**Try:**

- She moved with her usual grace.
- Poise was her default setting.
- As always, she remained unflustered.
- She handled the situation with the calm she was known for.
- Even under pressure, her composure never wavered.
- She had a natural elegance that defined her every move.

---

### Instead of: "his characteristic theatrical tone"

**Try:**

- He spoke, as he often did, with a flair for the dramatic.
- His voice boomed, filling the room in his usual way.
- He launched into the story, his hands gesturing with familiar energy.
- Theatrics were his native language.
- He couldn't resist adding a dramatic flourish to his words.
- His tone, ever the performer's, captured everyone's attention.

---

### Instead of: "his characteristic intensity"

**Try:**

- He fixed them with his usual focused gaze.
- As was his way, he leaned in, his focus absolute.
- The familiar fire ignited in his eyes.
- He approached the problem with the full force of his attention, as he always did.
- His presence had its usual weight, a gravity that pulled you in.

---

### Instead of: "her characteristic warmth"

**Try:**

- She smiled, and as always, it reached her eyes.
- Her voice was infused with its familiar kindness.
- She had a way of making everyone feel welcome, a warmth that was purely her.
- As was her habit, she offered a comforting hand.
- Her presence was a familiar comfort, like a hearth on a cold day.

---

### Instead of: "a characteristic hint of sarcasm"

**Try:**

- His words carried their usual ironic edge.
- A familiar smirk played on her lips.
- "Of course," he said, his voice dripping with its typical dryness.
- She answered with the wry tone he'd come to expect.
- Sarcasm was the default spice in her conversation.

---

### Instead of: "her characteristic analytical approach"

**Try:**

- She broke down the problem, as she always did, into its component parts.
- Her mind, ever the strategist's, was already mapping out the possibilities.
- She approached it with her typical logic, setting emotion aside.
- True to form, she had already created a list of pros and cons.
- Her instinct was to analyze, to understand every angle before acting.
