# Movement Descriptors

This guide provides rich, specific alternatives for describing character movement beyond basic verbs like "moved" or "went." The goal is to create vivid, character-specific motion that reveals personality, mood, and intention.

---

## Categories

### Rising/Standing Movement

- rose with theatrical flourish
- unfolded from the sectional with practiced grace
- bounced to her feet with characteristic enthusiasm
- swept upward in a single motion
- levered himself upward with surprising energy
- pushed off from his seat with genuine enthusiasm
- sprang up with natural movement
- rose with characteristic deliberateness
- emerged from his position with fluid ease
- lifted himself with measured intention

### Reaching/Extending Movement

- hand already reaching for Kayla's wrist
- extended her hand in gesture half-invitation, half-demand
- fingers already seeking contact
- arm stretching with inviting grace
- reached with practiced charm
- extended with coaxing motion
- gestured with familiar energy
- stretched with theatrical precision
- reached with characteristic intentionality

### Group/Social Movement

- drawing others into the moment
- creating an impromptu stage
- transforming invitation into performance
- rallying the group with infectious energy
- orchestrating spontaneous participation
- conducting the room's energy
- magnetizing attention through motion
- choreographing group dynamics

### Transitional Movement

- motion flowing like choreography
- movement transitioning seamlessly
- shifting with organic precision
- navigating the crowded space
- weaving through the gathering
- flowing between positions
- transitioning with characteristic grace
- moving with economy of motion

### Characteristic Movement Patterns

- measured movement reflecting formation
- geometric precision in positioning
- deliberate spacing and alignment
- calculated gesture and timing
- systematic approach to motion
- methodical navigation
- intentional positioning
- practiced efficiency in movement

## Show Don't Tell Examples

### Instead of: "She stood up to dance"

**Try:**

- Rise. Reach. Rally. The motion flowed like choreography—theatrical flourish transitioning to outstretched invitation.
- She swept upward with infectious enthusiasm, already tugging at the nearest wrist to join her impromptu performance.
- Prim rose with theatrical flourish, one hand already reaching for Kayla's wrist mid-mime into dance.
- She bounced to her feet with characteristic grace, her movement both commanding and coaxing.
- The standing was itself a performance—fluid, inviting, impossible to ignore.

---

### Instead of: "He moved across the room"

**Try:**

- He navigated the crowded room with an economy of motion, never jostling a soul.
- Jasper rose from the sectional with characteristic deliberateness, his movement measured and intentional.
- Every step was placed with geometric precision, as if he were walking on a sacred map.
- He moved with practiced efficiency, his path calculated to minimize disruption.
- His approach had the quality of careful choreography—purposeful yet graceful.

---

### Instead of: "They moved together"

**Try:**

- The group gathered in organic configuration, their positioning creating its own micro-climate.
- Bodies shifted and realigned like iron filings around a magnet.
- They flowed toward the common space, their movement synchronizing without conscious effort.
- The circle formed naturally, each person finding their place in the geometry of friendship.
- Movement became conversation—wordless negotiation of space and proximity.

---

### Instead of: "She gestured to him"

**Try:**

- Kayla pointed to Nate, signaling for him to join the silent disco.
- Her hand extended in invitation, bridging the space between observer and participant.
- She beckoned with the kind of gesture that transformed suggestion into expectation.
- Her motion carried the weight of inclusion—drawing him into the circle.
- The gesture was both request and rally, impossible to refuse.
