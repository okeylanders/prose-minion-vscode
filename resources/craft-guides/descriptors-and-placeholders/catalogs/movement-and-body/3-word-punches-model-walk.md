## Usage Examples

### Integrated into Prose

> She moved through the hallway with characteristic confidence: **stride-pose-strike**, each step a statement of presence.

> Prim's entrance was pure theater: **enter-elevate-enchant**, transforming the ordinary doorway into a stage.

> The way she crossed the room spoke volumes: **glide-grace-glow**, every gesture calculated for maximum impact.

> Her movement had runway precision: **measure-move-masterful**, as if invisible photographers were capturing every angle.

> When she turned to face them: **pivot-pose-powerful**, the room's attention shifted like iron filings to a magnet.

### Standalone Descriptors

- **Stride. Pose. Strike.** — for confident, commanding movement
- **Glide. Grace. Glow.** — for elegant, luminous presence
- **Enter. Elevate. Enchant.** — for dramatic entrances
- **Turn. Twist. Triumph.** — for pivotal moments
- **Own. Occupy. Overwhelm.** — for space-claiming movement

## Scene Applications

### Hallway Navigation
> **Pace-pause-perfect** — she navigated the institutional corridors as if they were her personal runway.

### Room Entrance
> **Arrive-attract-amaze** — the living room became her stage the moment she crossed the threshold.

### Social Positioning
> **Claim-command-conquer** — she found her place in the group configuration with the ease of someone accustomed to being watched.

### Transitional Moments
> **Flow-focus-fierce** — even the simple act of moving from couch to kitchen carried an undeniable presence.

### Group Dynamics
> **Direct-dazzle-divine** — her movement naturally drew others into her orbit.

## Notes on Usage

- Model walk sequences work best for confident characters or moments of character growth
- Can indicate both natural poise and cultivated confidence
- Effective for showing character dynamics and social positioning
- Use sparingly to maintain impact and avoid over-characterization
- Particularly effective in contrast with more casual movement styles
- Can show character development through progression from tentative to confident sequences
