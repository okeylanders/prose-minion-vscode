# Publishing Standards by Genre

A comprehensive reference guide for words per page, page dimensions, and formatting standards across different literary genres.

---

## Young Adult (YA) Fiction

**Words Per Page:** 250-300 words
**Common Page Sizes:**
- 5.5" × 8.5" (most common)
- 6" × 9"

**Typical Word Count Range:** 50,000-90,000 words
**Published Page Count:** 200-360 pages

**Formatting Notes:**
- Font: 11-12pt serif (Garamond, Minion Pro, Caslon)
- Line spacing: 1.2-1.4
- Margins: Standard (0.75"-1")
- Chapter breaks often include extra white space

**Literary Statistics:**
- **Unique Word Count:** 8,000-12,000 unique words
- **Lexical Density:** 50-55% (moderate vocabulary richness)
- **Dialogue Percentage:** 40-55% (high dialogue for teen engagement)
- **Avg Words Per Sentence:** 12-16 words
- **Avg Sentences Per Paragraph:** 4-6 sentences
- **Reading Time:** 4-6 hours (average reader at 250 wpm)
- **Chapter Count:** 20-35 chapters
- **Average Chapter Length:** 2,000-3,500 words
- **Word Length Distribution:**
  - 1-3 letters: 45-50%
  - 4-6 letters: 35-40%
  - 7+ letters: 10-15%

**Popular Examples:**
1. *The Hunger Games* by Suzanne Collins (101,564 words, 27 chapters)
2. *The Fault in Our Stars* by John Green (67,203 words, 25 chapters)

## Middle Grade Fiction

**Words Per Page:** 200-250 words
**Common Page Sizes:**
- 5.25" × 8"
- 5.5" × 8.25"

**Typical Word Count Range:** 20,000-55,000 words
**Published Page Count:** 100-275 pages

**Formatting Notes:**
- Font: 12-13pt serif (slightly larger for younger readers)
- Line spacing: 1.3-1.5 (more generous)
- Margins: Wider (0.85"-1.1")
- More illustrations or chapter art common

**Literary Statistics:**
- **Unique Word Count:** 4,000-7,000 unique words
- **Lexical Density:** 45-50% (simpler vocabulary for younger readers)
- **Dialogue Percentage:** 50-65% (very dialogue-heavy for accessibility)
- **Avg Words Per Sentence:** 10-14 words
- **Avg Sentences Per Paragraph:** 3-5 sentences
- **Reading Time:** 2-4 hours (average reader at 200 wpm)
- **Chapter Count:** 15-30 chapters
- **Average Chapter Length:** 1,200-2,500 words
- **Word Length Distribution:**
  - 1-3 letters: 50-55%
  - 4-6 letters: 35-40%
  - 7+ letters: 8-12%

**Popular Examples:**
1. *Wonder* by R.J. Palacio (73,053 words, 8 parts with multiple chapters)
2. *Percy Jackson & the Lightning Thief* by Rick Riordan (87,223 words, 22 chapters)

---

## Literary Fiction

**Words Per Page:** 250-350 words
**Common Page Sizes:**
- 5.5" × 8.25"
- 6" × 9"
- 5" × 8" (trade paperback)

**Typical Word Count Range:** 70,000-120,000 words
**Published Page Count:** 250-400 pages

**Formatting Notes:**
- Font: 10-11pt serif (often more compact)
- Line spacing: 1.15-1.3
- Margins: Standard to tight (0.7"-0.9")
- May include more experimental typography

**Literary Statistics:**
- **Unique Word Count:** 12,000-18,000 unique words
- **Lexical Density:** 55-65% (high vocabulary richness)
- **Dialogue Percentage:** 20-35% (more narrative/introspection)
- **Avg Words Per Sentence:** 16-22 words
- **Avg Sentences Per Paragraph:** 5-8 sentences
- **Reading Time:** 6-10 hours (average reader at 250 wpm)
- **Chapter Count:** 15-40 chapters (or no chapters)
- **Average Chapter Length:** 3,000-6,000 words
- **Word Length Distribution:**
  - 1-3 letters: 40-45%
  - 4-6 letters: 35-40%
  - 7+ letters: 18-25%

**Popular Examples:**
1. *The Goldfinch* by Donna Tartt (169,887 words, 12 chapters)
2. *All the Light We Cannot See* by Anthony Doerr (175,000 words, 173 short chapters)

## Romance

**Words Per Page:** 250-300 words
**Common Page Sizes:**
- 4.25" × 6.87" (mass market paperback)
- 5.5" × 8.5" (trade paperback)

**Typical Word Count Range:** 70,000-100,000 words
**Published Page Count:** 280-400 pages (mass market), 250-350 pages (trade)

**Formatting Notes:**
- Font: 9-10pt serif (mass market), 11-12pt (trade)
- Line spacing: 1.2-1.35
- Margins: Tight on mass market (0.5"-0.75"), standard on trade
- Category romance may be shorter (50,000-70,000 words)

**Literary Statistics:**
- **Unique Word Count:** 8,000-11,000 unique words
- **Lexical Density:** 48-53% (accessible vocabulary)
- **Dialogue Percentage:** 45-60% (high dialogue for relationship development)
- **Avg Words Per Sentence:** 13-17 words
- **Avg Sentences Per Paragraph:** 4-6 sentences
- **Reading Time:** 5-7 hours (average reader at 250 wpm)
- **Chapter Count:** 20-35 chapters
- **Average Chapter Length:** 2,500-4,000 words
- **Word Length Distribution:**
  - 1-3 letters: 45-50%
  - 4-6 letters: 35-40%
  - 7+ letters: 12-16%

**Popular Examples:**
1. *The Hating Game* by Sally Thorne (95,000 words, 23 chapters)
2. *Beach Read* by Emily Henry (102,000 words, 31 chapters)

## Thriller/Mystery

**Words Per Page:** 250-300 words
**Common Page Sizes:**
- 5.5" × 8.5"
- 6" × 9"
- 4.25" × 6.87" (mass market)

**Typical Word Count Range:** 70,000-100,000 words
**Published Page Count:** 280-400 pages

**Formatting Notes:**
- Font: 10-12pt serif
- Line spacing: 1.2-1.35
- Margins: Standard (0.75"-1")
- Short chapters common (creates page-turner effect)

**Literary Statistics:**
- **Unique Word Count:** 9,000-13,000 unique words
- **Lexical Density:** 50-56% (clear, propulsive vocabulary)
- **Dialogue Percentage:** 35-50% (balanced for pacing)
- **Avg Words Per Sentence:** 12-16 words
- **Avg Sentences Per Paragraph:** 3-5 sentences (shorter for tension)
- **Reading Time:** 5-8 hours (average reader at 250 wpm)
- **Chapter Count:** 30-60 chapters (short for page-turning)
- **Average Chapter Length:** 1,500-2,500 words
- **Word Length Distribution:**
  - 1-3 letters: 45-50%
  - 4-6 letters: 36-40%
  - 7+ letters: 12-16%

**Popular Examples:**
1. *Gone Girl* by Gillian Flynn (145,719 words, 67 chapters)
2. *The Girl on the Train* by Paula Hawkins (101,750 words, 78 chapters)

## Science Fiction/Fantasy

**Words Per Page:** 250-350 words
**Common Page Sizes:**
- 6" × 9" (most common for epic fantasy)
- 5.5" × 8.5"

**Typical Word Count Range:** 90,000-150,000+ words (can be much longer)
**Published Page Count:** 350-600+ pages

**Formatting Notes:**
- Font: 10-11pt serif (allows for longer works)
- Line spacing: 1.2-1.3
- Margins: Standard to tight (0.7"-0.9")
- May include maps, glossaries, appendices

**Literary Statistics:**
- **Unique Word Count:** 12,000-20,000 unique words (includes invented terms)
- **Lexical Density:** 52-60% (world-building vocabulary)
- **Dialogue Percentage:** 30-45% (balanced with description)
- **Avg Words Per Sentence:** 14-18 words
- **Avg Sentences Per Paragraph:** 4-7 sentences
- **Reading Time:** 8-15+ hours (average reader at 250 wpm)
- **Chapter Count:** 25-70 chapters
- **Average Chapter Length:** 3,000-5,000 words
- **Word Length Distribution:**
  - 1-3 letters: 42-47%
  - 4-6 letters: 35-40%
  - 7+ letters: 15-20%

**Popular Examples:**
1. *The Name of the Wind* by Patrick Rothfuss (259,000 words, 92 chapters)
2. *Ender's Game* by Orson Scott Card (100,609 words, 15 chapters)

- Line spacing: 1.2-1.4

- **Avg Sentences Per Paragraph:** 5-8 sentences
- **Reading Time:** 4-7 hours (average reader at 250 wpm)
- **Chapter Count:** 10-25 chapters
- **Average Chapter Length:** 3,000-6,000 words
- **Word Length Distribution:**
  - 1-3 letters: 40-45%

  - 7+ letters: 18-24%

**Popular Examples:**
1. *Educated* by Tara Westover (95,000 words, 40 chapters)

## Children's Picture Books

**Words Per Page:** 50-150 words (varies widely)
**Common Page Sizes:**
- 8" × 10"
- 8.5" × 11"
- 10" × 10" (square format)

**Typical Word Count Range:** 500-1,000 words (entire book)
**Published Page Count:** 24-40 pages

**Formatting Notes:**
- Font: 14-18pt (very large, readable)
- Text integrated with illustrations
- Significant white space for visual storytelling
- Read-aloud formatting considerations

**Literary Statistics:**
- **Unique Word Count:** 200-500 unique words
- **Lexical Density:** 40-50% (simple, repetitive vocabulary)
- **Dialogue Percentage:** 40-70% (read-aloud friendly)
- **Avg Words Per Sentence:** 6-10 words
- **Avg Sentences Per Paragraph:** 1-3 sentences
- **Reading Time:** 5-10 minutes (read-aloud)
- **Chapter Count:** N/A (continuous narrative)
- **Average Spread Length:** 25-50 words per page spread
- **Word Length Distribution:**
  - 1-3 letters: 60-70%
  - 4-6 letters: 25-35%
  - 7+ letters: 3-8%

**Popular Examples:**
1. *Where the Wild Things Are* by Maurice Sendak (338 words, 19 double-page spreads)
2. *The Very Hungry Caterpillar* by Eric Carle (224 words, 13 pages)

## Manuscript vs. Published Format

### Standard Manuscript Format (for submission)
- **Words Per Page:** ~250 words
- **Format:** 8.5" × 11"
- **Font:** 12pt Courier or Times New Roman
- **Spacing:** Double-spaced
- **Margins:** 1" all around

### Published Format Considerations
- Published books use tighter spacing and smaller fonts
- Trade paperbacks generally more spacious than mass market
- Hardcovers may use slightly larger fonts than paperbacks
- E-books: No fixed words-per-page (reflowable text)

## Notes on Variation

Words per page can vary significantly based on:
- **Typography choices**: Serif vs. sans-serif, condensed vs. regular
- **Paragraph formatting**: First-line indent vs. block paragraphs with space between
- **Chapter breaks**: More chapters = more white space = fewer words per page
- **Dialogue density**: Heavy dialogue scenes have more white space
- **Publisher standards**: Each publisher has house style guidelines
- **Target audience**: Younger readers get larger fonts and more space
- **Book design goals**: Perceived value, portability, reading experience
